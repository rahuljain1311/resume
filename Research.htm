Research

  • K. Drakopoulos, S. Jain, R. Randhawa, “Persuading Customers to Buy Early: The Value of Personalized Information Provisioning.” Under revision for Management Science.
We study the pricing & information provisioning game of a seller who is (ex-post) better informed about product availability. Using a Bayesian persuasion framework, we find that public information provisioning has limited value while personalized information provisioning is profitable, having attributes like personalized pricing.


Working Papers:
  • K. Drakopoulos, S. Jain, R. Randhawa, “Searching for an Infection in a Network”
We study the problem of finding an infected node in the least possible time in a given graph in which the infection spreads deterministically from an unknown infected node to its neighbours. We formulate the problem as an equivalent graph covering problem and develop an integer linear program for the same. We show that the problem is NP Hard for an arbitrary graph and derive polynomial time search policies for trees.
